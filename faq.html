The sanctuary houses a diverse range of bird species including parrots, toucans, owls, eagles, and various tropical birds native to the region.

Yes, we are proud to host several rare and endangered bird species as part of our conservation efforts. These include the Bali myna, California condor, and Philippine eagle.

While direct interaction with the birds is limited to ensure their well-being, visitors can enjoy observing them in their natural habitats throughout the sanctuary.

Our geckos play a vital role in the ecosystem of the sanctuary by helping control insect populations, including pests that may affect the bird habitats. They coexist peacefully with the birds, primarily inhabiting separate areas within the sanctuary. Our staff closely monitors their populations to ensure a balanced ecosystem.

Photography is permitted within designated areas of the sanctuary. However, flash photography is not allowed as it can startle the birds. We also request visitors to respect the privacy of the birds during nesting periods.
